We strive to accommodate ALL students who want to present at El Dia. That includes everyone with research-related work to share, from undergraduates up to late-stage graduate students. We hope to clarify the ways in which presenters can participate below:

    1. Oral (In-Person):
    • Required - 10-15 minute recorded presentation uploaded to TBA no later than March 16th
    • Required - 3-7 minute ‘lightning’ presentation with 1-4 slides followed by 5-7 minute Q&A delivered in-person at El Dia conference on March 22nd
    1. Oral (Online):
    • Required - 10-15 minute recorded presentation uploaded to TBA no later than March 16th
    • Required - 3-7 minute ‘lightning’ presentation with 1-4 slides followed by 5-7 minute Q&A delivered virtually SYNCHRONOUSLY VIA ZOOM to El Dia conference on March 22nd
    1. Poster (In-Person):
    • Required - 60 minute poster session delivered in-person at El Dia conference onMarch 22nd
    • Optional - upload PDF and/or 5-15 minute recorded presentation of poster to TBA no later than March 16th
    • Acceptable Poster Sizes: Up to Dimensions of 36" x 54"
    • Poster Template
    • Poster Printing
    1. **NEW CATEGORY** Asynchronous Virtual Research Snippet:
    • Required - 5-15 minute recorded presentation uploaded to TBA no later than March 16th
    • Note: This content will be accessible on the website, but not shared synchronously at the El Dia conference on March 22.
  • Which Presentation Should I Choose?
    • I have results completed for a research project: apply for a virtual or an in-person oral presentation!
    • I have research in progress or a research idea: apply for a poster presentation!
    • I can’t make the in-person conference, don’t feel up to an oral presentation, and have some in-progress research and/or ideas I would like to share: apply for a apply for an asynchronous research snippet!
  • Judging:
    • Virtual and In-Person Oral presentations will be formally judged in one category, and open to awards.
    • In-Person Poster presentations will be formally judged in another category, and open to awards.
    • Self-paced, asynchronous virtual research snippet will not be formally judged, and thus are not open to awards.
  • Professional Development Requirement:
    • Presenting in a judged category (Oral & Poster) fulfills the HAS professional development requirement for graduation. Non-judged categories (i.e. the research snippet) will not fulfill the HAS professional development requirement.
    • Final Notes:
      • There will be no more formal changes to these categories.
      • Please keep in mind that due to scheduling you may not get your first choice.
      • The exact length of the March 22 oral presentations will depend on the length and number of submissions.
    We realize optimal solutions for everyone’s preferences don’t exist. However, we want to be as inclusive as we can be. If there is any doubt about which category to choose, either a) contact has.student.symposium@gmail.com, or b) list preferences and concerns in abstract submission form.

    REMINDER: El Dia is a professional symposium and presenting at El Dia fulfills the professional development requirement for graduation.
